About the products
Water bottles are made of high quality borosilicate glass and also contain gemstones.
In japanese, ishimizu means "stone water" or "water from stones"
The brand draws inspiration from Japanese culture and from it's beautiful nature. In particular mountain torrents where pure water flows and polishes natural stones

Other than proposing a very nice and appealing object, the bottles are meant to appeal to people believing in the energies and properties of different types of stones.
Some people believe the stone will have an effect on the beverage contained in the bottle.
This needs to be emphasized in an indirect and subtle way.
Also, gemstones are not directly in contact with the liquid in the bottle and can be changed or replaced easily.

3 model lines of bottles :
Heiwa (means peace) : single wall glass water bottle with gemstone gravels in a bell-shaped chamber
Kanjo (means emotion) : single wall water bottle with gemstone pebbles in a container at the base of the bottle
Hogo (means protection) : double wall water bottle with gemstone pebbles in a container at the base of the bottle
